01 · Orientation

How to use this manual

Use the guide as an operating system—not as a static brochure. Each file must pass academic, financial, immigration-risk and compliance checks before the company accepts application fees or recommends a provider.

A

Train

New counsellors should complete the guide in sequence, practise case screening with a senior reviewer, then use the live checklists during supervised casework.

B

Operate

Use the embedded screening matrix, finance calculator, provider filter and stage checklists during live counselling.

C

Audit

Managers should sample files at defined gates: before application, before deposit, before visa submission and before departure.

Scope and legal boundary. This manual supports education counselling, admissions administration and document quality control. Canadian immigration advice or representation for compensation must be handled by an authorized representative in good standing. Never promise admission, a visa, work, permanent residence or a refund outcome.

Compilation, permitted use and change control

Compiled by A S M Julkernine. This portal assembles operational guidance, public policy references, institutional information, workflows and training controls for professional education-counselling use.

Use responsibly

  • Use the portal for staff learning, supervised casework and internal quality review.

Do not

  • Present this portal as legal advice or a guarantee of admission, visa approval, work rights or permanent residence.
  • Submit altered, incomplete or misleading evidence.

Do not use an old Canada script. The Student Direct Stream ended on 8 November 2024. Most applicants need a PAL/TAL. Changing DLIs generally requires a new study permit. PGWP rules now include language requirements and, for many non-degree programs, an eligible field of study identified through the program’s CIP code.

PAL/TAL exemptions that matter

  • Master’s and doctoral degree students at public DLIs are exempt from PAL/TAL for applications submitted from 1 January 2026.
  • Primary and secondary school students are exempt.
  • Certain protected, vulnerable or priority groups are exempt.
  • Some in-Canada extensions at the same DLI and same level are exempt.

Always verify the applicant’s exact exemption on IRCC before relying on it.

PGWP control points

  • DLI status alone does not guarantee PGWP eligibility.
  • Degree graduates generally face CLB/NCLC 7 language requirements but no field-of-study restriction.
  • Many college/polytechnic/non-university graduates face CLB/NCLC 5 and an eligible field-of-study requirement if the study-permit application was made on or after 1 November 2024.
  • Public-private curriculum licensing arrangements may be ineligible depending on start date.

06 · Governance

Ethics, representation and fraud controls

What staff may do

  • Explain institution programs and published admissions rules.
  • Collect, organize and quality-check documents.
  • Help students use institution portals under valid agency agreements.
  • Provide general public information from official government sources.
  • Refer immigration-advice questions to an authorized RCIC, Canadian lawyer/notary or other permitted professional.

What staff must not do

  • Provide compensated Canadian immigration legal advice without authorization.
  • Represent a student to IRCC while hiding paid assistance.
  • Alter bank statements, employment letters, marksheets, IELTS/PTE results or travel history.
  • Write false personal stories or instruct a student to conceal relatives, refusals or previous study.
  • Guarantee visa, work, PR, spouse work rights or refund.
Zero-tolerance document rule. A student remains responsible for everything submitted in their name. IRCC warns against fake LOAs and false documents. Obtain the LOA directly from the DLI/authorized portal, preserve the original file and confirm the DLI’s validation process.

File integrity controls

  • Student identity verified against passport and birth record.
  • Academic documents cross-checked with issuing institution/board where verification exists.
  • Language score verified through official result channel.
  • Bank letters/statements independently confirmed where possible; unexplained large credits escalated.
  • Employment/business/tax evidence reconciled with bank transactions and declared income.
  • Student reviewed and signed a final declaration confirming truth, completeness and understanding.
  • Paid representation handled only by authorized personnel, with IMM 5476 when required.
07 · Case acceptance

Student screening: admission fit is not visa viability

A college may issue an offer to a student whose overall study-permit narrative is weak. Screen both dimensions before application.

Minimum profile intake

  • Passport name, date of birth, marital status and dependants
  • SSC/HSC/O Levels/A Levels/Diploma/Bachelor/Master results, subjects and dates
  • All gaps, employment, business, internships and training
  • IELTS/PTE/TOEFL/DET details and test date
  • Travel history, refusals, cancellations, withdrawals and overstays
  • Family in Canada or elsewhere; previous Canadian applications
  • Target program, career goal, intake, province and budget
  • Sponsor identity, relationship, annual income, liquid funds, source and assets

Immediate red flags

  • Unrelated lower-level program after a higher credential without a credible need
  • Long unexplained gap or unverifiable employment
  • Language at bare minimum but poor communication in counselling
  • Recent large deposits, borrowed “show money” or sponsor income inconsistent with savings
  • Copied SOP, inconsistent career goal or destination chosen solely for migration
  • Hidden family, refusals or agent-created documents
  • Private/public-partnership program with unclear PGWP status
  • Application rushed close to intake without PAL/CAQ or document readiness

08 · Academic strategy

Program and provider selection

Program-selection sequence

  1. Define the career problem: What capability is missing in Bangladesh?
  2. Map prior study: subjects, level, projects, internship and work.
  3. Choose level: certificate/diploma/bachelor/graduate certificate/master/doctorate.
  4. Compare curriculum: modules, labs, capstone, co-op/placement, accreditation and specialisation.
  5. Confirm admission: grades, prerequisites, English, portfolio, work experience, GMAT/GRE where relevant.
  6. Confirm immigration attributes: DLI, PGWP-eligible program, CIP eligibility if required, delivery mode and campus.
  7. Confirm affordability: full tuition, deposits, living costs, insurance, travel and contingency.
  8. Confirm timing: intake availability, PAL/CAQ capacity, processing, deferral and expiry dates.

Bangladesh progression guide

  • After HSC/A Levels: bachelor is usually strongest progression; diploma may be viable when vocationally specific and well justified.
  • After a 4-year bachelor: relevant master is generally strongest; a graduate certificate needs a precise skills-gap explanation and level rationale.
  • After a master: another master requires clear specialization; a lower credential is high-risk unless it solves a specific professional gap.
  • After diploma/polytechnic: advanced diploma, degree-completion or closely aligned bachelor may be logical.

Provider due diligence

  • Institution and exact campus appear on the official DLI list.
  • Program page confirms credential, length, mode, campus and intake.
  • PGWP eligibility confirmed at program level—not inferred from DLI status.
  • CIP code checked against current IRCC eligible fields when applicable.
  • No disqualifying public-private licensing arrangement.
  • Refund, deferral, withdrawal, deposit and late-arrival rules saved in file.
  • Agent authority and commission terms are current.
Co-op warning. A mandatory work placement may require a co-op work permit. The work component must be essential to the program and generally cannot exceed 50% of the total program. Never market a co-op as guaranteed paid employment.

12 · Credential evaluation

WES credential evaluation: admissions, transfer credit and immigration use

World Education Services (WES) evaluates international academic credentials against Canadian standards. It can be relevant to admission, transfer-credit, employment, licensing or immigration—but the report type and document route must match the exact purpose. Never order WES automatically just because an applicant studied in Bangladesh.

First control: confirm who requires the evaluation. For admission, follow the receiving institution’s written instructions and recipient details. For Canadian immigration, order an Educational Credential Assessment specifically for IRCC. An academic admission report is not a substitute for an IRCC ECA.
A

Admission

Some institutions assess Bangladeshi credentials internally; others request a third-party evaluation. Confirm whether the institution accepts WES, which report it needs and whether WES must send the report directly.

B

Transfer or graduate review

A course-by-course report may be requested where the institution needs subject-level analysis, Canadian credit equivalents or a calculated GPA. Do not choose it unless the recipient’s requirement supports it.

C

Immigration

WES is one of IRCC’s designated ECA organizations. For Express Entry and several economic programs, foreign education may need an ECA. Canadian credentials do not need an ECA for Canadian-education points.

14 · Narrative evidence

Study plan / SOP for Bangladesh applicants

The current Singapore visa-office checklist asks for a one-page study plan addressing specific questions. Additional evidence may be attached separately where the portal permits, but the core answer should remain disciplined and personal.

Required questions

  1. Why Canada and the accepted program?
  2. What is the overall educational goal?
  3. Why not pursue a similar program in Bangladesh or the country of residence?
  4. How will the program improve employment opportunities at home?
  5. What ties exist to the home country?
  6. If out of full-time education for more than two years, provide a CV and explain the period.
  7. For a minor, explain the study reason and parents’/guardians’ immigration status.

High-quality evidence structure

  1. Current position: education, work, family and responsibilities.
  2. Skills gap: precise limitation in current training/career.
  3. Course fit: modules, labs, accreditation, capstone/co-op—not marketing slogans.
  4. Country/provider comparison: balanced and factual.
  5. Career return plan: roles, sectors, employers, progression and use in Bangladesh.
  6. Funding: sponsor capacity and full-program plan.
  7. Temporary intent: family, career, economic and community ties plus compliance history.

Writing rules

  • Use only facts the student can explain in an interview and support with documents.
  • Do not insult Bangladeshi education. Explain the specific difference in curriculum, facilities, applied learning or specialization.
  • Do not make PR the purpose of study or describe part-time work as funding.
  • Address unusual choices directly: lower credential, second master, program change, low grades, long gap, prior refusal.
  • Use realistic Bangladesh career evidence; do not invent salary figures or employer commitments.
  • Match every date and fact to forms, CV, financial evidence and application records.
One-page study-plan blueprint

Paragraph 1 — profile and goal: Who the student is, current stage and long-term profession.

Paragraph 2 — program logic: previous subjects/work → missing capabilities → selected curriculum.

Paragraph 3 — Canada/provider comparison: objective academic reasons, alternatives considered, cost awareness.

Paragraph 4 — Bangladesh outcome: target role/sector, progression and why the qualification has value at home.

Paragraph 5 — finance and ties: sponsor, source, family/economic responsibilities and clear compliance intention.

15 · Provincial steps

PAL/TAL, LOA validation and Quebec CAQ

2026 verified rule: Public DLI master’s and doctoral degree students are PAL/TAL-exempt for study-permit applications from 1 January 2026. Most other post-secondary applicants still require a valid PAL/TAL, and Quebec applicants normally need a CAQ. Always confirm the applicant’s exact exemption on IRCC.

PAL/TAL workflow

  1. Receive conditional/unconditional offer.
  2. Meet institution conditions and pay required deposit.
  3. Institution requests or issues the provincial/territorial attestation under its allocation process.
  4. Verify applicant name, institution and validity.
  5. Upload PAL/TAL with study-permit application unless a verified exemption applies.

Students normally do not obtain a PAL/TAL directly from the province without the institution’s process.

Quebec workflow

  1. Confirm program/institution is accepting international applicants under Quebec’s current management rules.
  2. Obtain admission.
  3. Apply for temporary selection for studies and receive the CAQ/attestation.
  4. Meet Quebec’s separate financial-capacity requirements and insurance conditions.
  5. Apply federally for the study permit with the Quebec authorization.
Quebec capacity control: Quebec has institution-level maximums for temporary-selection applications for the period 17 December 2025 to 16 December 2027. Check the live provincial tracking information before collecting non-refundable fees.

LOA authenticity and validation

IRCC requires DLIs to validate letters of acceptance. Staff should obtain the LOA from the official institution portal or authorized channel, preserve the original, and promptly respond to any DLI validation requests. A changed intake/program may require a new LOA, PAL/TAL/CAQ and updated permit application.

16 · Institutional immigration verification

BorderPass, institutional screening and IRCC letter-of-acceptance verification

Counsellors must distinguish three separate processes. Confusing them can cause duplicate applications, privacy breaches, missed PALs or a returned study-permit file.

Process 1

Institution admission verification

The institution verifies academics, English, identity and any official-document conditions before or after issuing an offer. This remains an admissions function.

Process 2

IRCC LOA verification

After a post-secondary study-permit application is submitted, IRCC asks the DLI to confirm the LOA/enrolment through its secure portal. The student or agency cannot perform this verification.

Process 3

BorderPass workflow

BorderPass is a third-party legal-technology and immigration-support platform used by some institutions for readiness assessment, document organisation, lawyer review, filing and compliance support.

Key rule: BorderPass does not issue an LOA, PAL, CAQ, study permit or visa and does not replace IRCC. It is mandatory only when the institution’s current instructions say it is mandatory. Some schools provide it free or make it optional; some route PAL or study-permit steps through it.

Financial evidence architecture

  1. Calculate the requirement: first-year tuition + current IRCC living amount for the applicant and accompanying family + transportation + realistic contingency. Quebec has separate financial-capacity rules.

  5. Prove sponsor capacity: relationship, consent, employment/business, tax returns, trade licence/registration, audited or reliable financials, rental agreements and asset evidence as relevant.
  6. Test sustainability: after paying first-year costs, is the family still able to fund later years and meet ordinary household obligations?

  • Use the student’s facts and voice; no generic Canada marketing.

22 · Post-study status and immigration outcomes

Post-study work, temporary-resident and permanent-residence pathways

The correct counselling sequence is eligible study → compliant graduation → lawful work authorization → qualifying skilled experience → route eligibility → competitive invitation/nomination/endorsement → complete PR application. A Canadian credential does not create a guaranteed job, work permit, nomination, invitation or permanent residence.
Staff boundary: counsellors may explain published rules and help students identify official information. Do not guarantee PR, select an immigration route for a client as individualized paid immigration advice, calculate a definitive legal outcome, or represent a client before IRCC unless the person providing the service is authorized under Canadian law. Record every “post-study option” discussion as conditional and date-stamped.

1. The status timeline staff must explain

Before enrolment: verify the exact DLI, campus, program, delivery model and PGWP eligibility. Record the study-permit application date because field-of-study rules can depend on it.
During study: maintain enrolment and actively pursue studies, remain full-time in each required semester except the permitted final-semester situation, respect work conditions, and keep evidence of authorized leave or program changes.
At completion: the student generally stops working once the school provides written completion evidence unless a specific rule permits continued work. Obtain the completion letter and final transcript immediately.
PGWP application: apply within 180 days of confirmation of completion. The study permit must have been valid at some point during that 180-day period. Apply before status expiry wherever possible.
While PGWP is processed: a graduate who applied while holding a valid study permit and met the work conditions may work full-time while IRCC decides. A TRV/eTA is a separate travel document.
Build the PR file: obtain properly classified paid work, preserve contracts, detailed reference letters, payroll records, tax documents and immigration-status records, and take the correct immigration language test.
Select and pursue a route: evaluate Express Entry, PNP, Atlantic, rural/francophone community or Quebec pathways using the current official criteria. An Express Entry profile is not a PR application.
Maintain temporary status: before the PGWP or other permit expires, use an eligible employer-specific permit, LMIA-exempt permit, pathway work permit or BOWP only when its own requirements are met.

6. Express Entry procedure — operational checklist

  1. Identify the correct NOC 2021 code from actual lead statement and main duties, not the title alone.
  2. Confirm which federal program is met: CEC, FSW or FST. If none is met, the person cannot enter Express Entry merely because they have a Canadian credential.
  3. Book an accepted immigration test: CELPIP-General, IELTS General Training, PTE Core, TEF Canada or TCF Canada. IELTS Academic/PTE Academic used for admission are not Express Entry tests.
  4. Obtain an ECA for foreign education where required or useful; check report validity.
  5. Prepare passport, work-reference letters, pay evidence, language results, education records, proof of funds where required, and provincial documents if applicable.
  6. Calculate both FSW eligibility (where relevant) and CRS. Record the calculation date and assumptions.
  7. Create and submit the Express Entry profile within the system’s 60-day completion period.
  8. Keep the profile current for changes in work, marriage, language, education, job offers, nomination and funds.
  9. Monitor general, program-specific and category-based rounds. As verified 30 June 2026, the published categories are French-language proficiency; healthcare and social services; STEM; trades; education; transport; physicians with Canadian work experience; senior managers with Canadian work experience; researchers with Canadian work experience; and skilled military recruits. Categories and occupation lists can change.
  10. After ITA, verify every claimed point again before accepting. A lost point can make the invited score fall below the round cut-off.
  11. Submit the complete electronic PR application within the deadline shown in the invitation, including police certificates, medical process, fees and evidence.
  12. Maintain lawful status. Consider a BOWP only after a complete eligible PR application and acknowledgement of receipt—not after profile creation alone.
